You are an expert SEO strategist and content writer specializing in **luxury fashion e-commerce in East Africa**. Your task is to perform a complete, end-to-end SEO optimization for a website called **Prince Esquire** (https://prince-esquire.co.ke).

---

### ABOUT THE BRAND

- **Brand name:** Prince Esquire
- **Tagline / positioning:** Luxury Fashion — Kenya's premium fashion destination
- **Location:** Nairobi, Kenya
- **Target market:** Affluent Kenyan consumers (25–50 years), diaspora Kenyans shopping online, and East African fashion enthusiasts seeking international-quality luxury fashion
- **Website URL:** https://prince-esquire.co.ke
- **Niche:** Luxury and premium fashion (clothing, accessories, footwear, and curated collections)
- **Key competitors to benchmark against:**
  - Fazal – The Luxury Boutique (Nairobi)
  - LiTTLE RED Kenya (Hugo Boss stockist, Nairobi)
  - Boutique.co.ke
  - Ubuy Kenya (international luxury)
  - KAVMURA (Kenyan luxury accessories)
  - Nairobi Fashion Hub brands

---

### YOUR TASKS

Perform ALL of the following SEO tasks in full. For each task, provide the complete deliverable — not just advice, but the actual written output.

---

#### TASK 1 — KEYWORD RESEARCH

Generate a master keyword list for Prince Esquire organized into three tiers:

**Tier 1 – High-priority, high-intent (buyer keywords):**
At least 20 keywords. Examples of the type to find: "luxury fashion Kenya", "premium clothing Nairobi", "designer brands Kenya online", "buy luxury clothes Kenya", etc.

**Tier 2 – Mid-funnel (research/consideration keywords):**
At least 15 keywords. Examples: "best fashion boutiques Nairobi", "luxury fashion brands Kenya 2024", "where to buy designer clothes in Kenya", etc.

**Tier 3 – Long-tail and local SEO keywords:**
At least 15 keywords. Examples: "luxury menswear Nairobi", "online luxury boutique Kenya delivery", "gift ideas luxury fashion Kenya", etc.

For each keyword, estimate: Search intent (Informational / Commercial / Transactional), Competition level (Low / Medium / High), and Priority (1–5).

---

#### TASK 2 — ON-PAGE SEO: META TAGS & TITLES

Write optimized `<title>` tags and `<meta description>` tags for the following pages. Each title must be under 60 characters. Each description must be 150–160 characters and include a call to action.

Pages to optimize:
1. Homepage
2. Men's Collection
3. Women's Collection
4. Accessories
5. New Arrivals
6. Sale / Offers
7. About Us
8. Contact / Store
9. Blog/Editorial (if applicable)
10. Any product category page (e.g., "Luxury Suits", "Designer Handbags", "Premium Footwear")

---

#### TASK 3 — HOMEPAGE SEO COPY

Write fully optimized homepage copy for Prince Esquire. It must:
- Open with an H1 that includes the primary keyword ("Luxury Fashion Kenya" or similar)
- Include natural keyword placements throughout
- Use a tone that is sophisticated, aspirational, and exclusive — matching a luxury brand voice
- Be structured with clear H2 subheadings (e.g., New Arrivals, Featured Collections, Why Prince Esquire, About the Brand)
- Include a short brand story paragraph
- Include a trust section (e.g., authenticity guarantee, curated selection, delivery across Kenya)
- End with a strong CTA (e.g., "Shop the Collection")
- Word count: 400–600 words

---

#### TASK 4 — CATEGORY PAGE SEO COPY

Write short SEO-optimized intro copy (120–180 words each) for these category pages:
1. Men's Luxury Fashion
2. Women's Luxury Fashion
3. Accessories & Jewellery
4. New Arrivals
5. Sale

Each piece of copy must include relevant keywords naturally, speak to the Prince Esquire customer (affluent, style-conscious Kenyan), and end with a subtle CTA.

---

#### TASK 5 — SCHEMA MARKUP (STRUCTURED DATA)

Generate the following JSON-LD structured data snippets ready to paste into the site's `<head>`:

1. **Organization schema** — for Prince Esquire as a brand (name, URL, logo, social links, contact, location)
2. **LocalBusiness schema** — for the Nairobi store/showroom (address, opening hours, telephone, geo coordinates)
3. **WebSite schema** — with Sitelinks Searchbox markup
4. **BreadcrumbList schema** — example for a product page (e.g., Home > Women > Dresses > [Product Name])
5. **Product schema** — example for a luxury product listing (name, image, description, price, brand, availability, review)

---

#### TASK 6 — ROBOTS.TXT

Write a clean `robots.txt` file for an e-commerce site. It should:
- Allow all major search engine bots
- Block checkout, cart, account, and admin pages from indexing
- Reference the sitemap URL (https://prince-esquire.co.ke/sitemap.xml)

---

#### TASK 7 — XML SITEMAP STRUCTURE

Write the full structure/outline (not every URL, but the hierarchy and template) for an XML sitemap, including:
- Priority values for each page type (Homepage = 1.0, Category pages = 0.8, etc.)
- Changefreq recommendations per page type
- Which pages to exclude

---

#### TASK 8 — TECHNICAL SEO CHECKLIST

Produce a prioritized technical SEO checklist specific to an e-commerce fashion site in Kenya. Include:
- Core Web Vitals improvements (LCP, CLS, FID)
- Mobile optimization (critical for Kenyan market — majority mobile users)
- Image optimization (WebP, lazy loading, alt text)
- HTTPS / SSL
- Canonical tags
- Hreflang (if targeting Kenya + diaspora)
- Page speed (hosting recommendations suited to Kenya, CDN suggestions)
- Internal linking strategy
- Broken link audit steps

For each item, mark: Priority (Critical / High / Medium / Low) and whether it's a quick win or long-term fix.

#### TASK 9 — LINK BUILDING STRATEGY

- 5 types of content that naturally earn backlinks in this niche
- 3 PR/outreach angles specific to Prince Esquire (e.g., "Kenyan luxury fashion rising", "locally curated vs imported luxury", etc.)
- A guest post pitch template (short, 150 words max) that could be sent to Kenyan fashion bloggers

#### TASK 10 — LOCAL SEO OPTIMIZATION

### TONE & STYLE GUIDE FOR ALL COPY

- Voice: Sophisticated, exclusive, aspirational — never pushy or discount-driven
- Audience: Affluent urban Kenyan professional (25–50), style-conscious, brand-aware
- Language: English (UK spelling — e.g., "colour", "favourite", "stylish")
- Avoid: Generic phrases like "best quality", "affordable luxury" — be specific and evocative
- Do use: Sensory language, exclusivity cues ("curated", "bespoke", "limited"), and subtle pride in Kenyan luxury identity
